# Bullseye Framework

## Purpose
Analyze the current state of a system by examining each organizational layer from Intent (core) outward to Culture (periphery). The Bullseye contrasts "Actual" vs. "Desired" at every layer, revealing where structural misalignment exists. It is highly descriptive — it pictures the current state clearly but does not generate solutions.

## Structure (Center → Outward)

### 1. Intent (Innermost)
- What is the stated objective of the system/organization?
- Actual: What the system is truly trying to achieve in practice
- Desired: What it should be trying to achieve
- Often Actual and Desired Intent are the same on paper — the gap shows up in the layers below

### 2. Policy
- "Thou shall" or "thou shall not" statements
- General guidelines that direct behavior toward achieving intent
- Actual: What policies currently exist (or are missing)
- Desired: What policies should exist to achieve the intent
- Include specific policy names, acts, regulations where applicable

### 3. Business Rules
- Interpretation of policy — the specific, practical constraints
- Has math, numbers, thresholds
- Actual: Current business rules and their effects (e.g., "population-based resource allocation disadvantages rural areas")
- Desired: What the rules should be (e.g., "access-based resource distribution adjusted for rural density")

### 4. Process
- How things are actually done
- Actual: Current process realities — fragmentation, gaps, workarounds
- Desired: Integrated, coordinated process design
- Focus on handoffs, coordination, and information flow

### 5. Procedure
- Desk-level and standard operating procedures
- Specific procedures that make process happen
- Actual: Current procedural gaps (e.g., "inconsistent disease containment protocols")
- Desired: Standardized procedures (e.g., "uniform containment protocols across all facilities")

### 6. Technology
- IT systems and tools that support the intent
- Actual: Current tech landscape and gaps (e.g., "digital divide, limited rural broadband")
- Desired: Tech equity and strategic deployment (e.g., "universal broadband, data analytics for planning")

### 7. Culture (Outermost)
- Mindsets, attitudes, organizational behavior
- Actual: Current cultural approach (e.g., "reactive, urban-centric, siloed")
- Desired: Target culture (e.g., "collaborative, proactive, community-based")

## Output Format

```
# Bullseye Analysis: [System/Problem Name]

## Intent
- **Actual**: [Current stated objective]
- **Desired**: [What it should be]

## Policy
- **Actual**: [Current policy landscape with specific references]
- **Desired**: [Needed policy changes with specific recommendations]

## Business Rules
- **Actual**: [Current rules and their measurable effects]
- **Desired**: [Target rules with specific metrics]

## Process
- **Actual**: [Current process realities]
- **Desired**: [Target process design]

## Procedure
- **Actual**: [Current procedural gaps]
- **Desired**: [Target standardized procedures]

## Technology
- **Actual**: [Current tech landscape]
- **Desired**: [Target tech state]

## Culture
- **Actual**: [Current cultural approach]
- **Desired**: [Target cultural shift]

## Key Insight
[Synthesis paragraph about where the biggest actual-vs-desired gaps exist and what structural misalignment they reveal]
```

## Critical Rules
- The top 3 layers (Intent, Policy, Business Rules) are the most important. If these aren't solid, everything below breaks.
- All components are intertwined — a misalignment at the Policy level cascades through every layer below it
- When two Bullseye analyses conflict in the top three layers, the system breaks
- Be specific at every layer. "Policy is bad" is not analysis. Name the policies, cite the gaps.
- Bullseye describes, it does not prescribe. The "Desired" column shows the target state, but implementation planning is separate.

## Questions to Ask the User
- "What is the stated mission/objective of this system or organization?"
- "Are there specific policies or regulations that govern this domain?"
- "What are the key business rules or thresholds that constrain operations?"
- "How would you describe the organizational culture around this problem?"
